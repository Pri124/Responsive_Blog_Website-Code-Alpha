About B.R. Ambedkar

B.R. Ambedkar, also known as Babasaheb Ambedkar, was born on April 14, 1891, in Mhow, a small town in present-day Madhya Pradesh, India.

He was an advocate of social justice, equality, and the rights of the marginalized communities. Ambedkar played a crucial role in the formation of modern India's Constitution, which enshrines the principles of justice, liberty, equality, and fraternity.

Ambedkar was also the first Law Minister of independent India. He dedicated his life to fighting against caste discrimination and uplifting the oppressed communities.
